Film Name: Nirontor (নিরন্তর) (Forever Flows), http://imdb.com/title/tt0872123/
Country of origin: Bangladesh
Runtime: 104 Minutes
Language: Bengali (with English Subtitle)
Cast: Shabnoor, Ilyas Kanchan.

Plot summary: Based on a famous novel written by contemporary popular writer Humayun Ahmed, the film portrays the  struggle of a lower middle class family. A lower-middle class family in Bangladesh's capital city of Dhaka is going through hard times. Their father has gone blind and can no longer work, and most of the family's money has been wasted on their unemployed son Hiru, who refuses to work. All the family's problems fall on the shoulder of their beautiful daughter Tithi, who works as a prostitute to help the family. The film is a fairly non-judgmental look at the lives of Tithi, her family and the people she comes in contact with through her job.

Film Name: Caravan
Country of origin: Nepal
Runtime: 90 Minutes
Language: Dolpo-language - closer to Tibetean (with English Subtitle)

Plot summary: Caravan is the film prepared by Erik Valley, French adventure traveler. It was finally released in November 1999 but took more than 3 years to visualize its scenes. It is based on the real life of upper Dolpo, extremely remote place of Nepal. Caravan has been rounded up with group journey of Dolpo people to Tibet for carrying salt. It might reflect the most severe lifestyle in the world how people have been struggling with rough mountain landscape.  Main Actor of the film is Thinle, an old man of 72 years, who was supposed to leading the group journey to Tibet for salt.

It became very popular among Nepalese audience during its show in Katmandu and established the new dimension of film and documentaries.  Caravan is the first film prepared in Tibetean origin language in Nepal.
 
 

Time: 4.30 PM
Film Name: Flåklypa Grand Prix, (Pinchcliffe Grand Prix) http://imdb.com/title/tt0073000/
Country of origin: Norway
Runtime: 88 Minutes
Language: Norwegian (with English Subtitle)

Plot summary: 
In the town of Flåklypa, the inventor Reodor Felgen lives with his animal friends Ludvig (a nervous, pessimistic and melancholic porcupine) and Solan (a cheerful and optimistic bird). Reodor works as a bicycle repairman, though he spends most of his time inventing weird Rube Goldberg-like contraptions. One day, the trio discover that one of Reodor's former assistants, Rudolf Blodstrupmoen, has stolen his design for a race car engine and has become a world champion Formula 1 driver. Solan secures funding from an Arab oil sheik who happens to be vacationing in Flåklypa, and to enter the race, the trio builds a gigantic racing car: II Tempo Gigante.

Flåklypa Grand Prix (released under the English title "Pinchcliffe Grand Prix") is a Norwegian stop motion-animated feature film directed by Ivo Caprino. It was released in 1975 and is based on characters from a series of books by Norwegian cartoonist and author Kjell Aukrust. It is the most widely-seen  Norwegian film of all time, having sold some 5.5 million tickets since its release to a population which currently numbers just 4.7 million.
