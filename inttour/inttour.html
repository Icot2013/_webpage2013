Currency:

The three countries have all different currencies.
  - So you need 3 different type of currencies with you, as you have to buy food and when you shop for small amount you may need to use local currency.
  - I hope all of you have credit/debit cards, so you can use them while shopping.
  - Using credit card for shopping, in most of the cases have no charge.
  - There are ATMs all over the cities to take out money. But your bank may charge approx. 30 NOK each time you use your card in ATM
  - You can exchange money in the cruise or in any money exchange. They may charge you some tax.
  - You can also buy these currencies from Norwegian banks. But they charge around 70 NOK each time you exchange currency (I am not sure, you ask your bank).
  - Its not a good idea to bring all money in cash, in case you lost it or pickpocket or in the worst case robbed.
  - One thing I can recommend is that, you can take 150 NOK per day as cash, so 5 day, 750 NOK. When you go to Sweden, Estonia or Latvia, you can exchange their currency against NOK. You can take euro, dollar or TC, but it not wise to buy those here and exchange there... as you know each time you exchange currency you have some loss. I can tell about Sweden, the official exchange authority is Forex, and they have branches in large markets, esp one in T-Centralen, the central train/metro/bus station. So we can get Swedish SEK kronor there.
  - Current Norwegian currency rate is available at https://www.postbanken.no/portal/biztools/valutakalkulator/valutakurser.jhtml
